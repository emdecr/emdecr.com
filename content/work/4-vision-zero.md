A research project in partnership with the City of Toronto's Vision Zero road safety initiative, completed as part of the inaugural cohort of the UX Research Collective's Intro to UX Research program.

I chose to focus on newcomer pedestrians — people who had arrived in Canada within the past five years — and how their perceptions of road safety evolved from arrival through settlement.

**Key work:**

- Recruited 10 participants and conducted hour-long qualitative interviews across homes, libraries, community centres, and parks
- Performed thematic analysis on transcribed interviews, identifying a journey from hyper-vigilance to normalization of risk
- Reframed findings from thematic clusters into a journey model mapping perception and behavioural change over time
- Surfaced actionable insights around earlier intervention and missed touchpoints in newcomer programming

**What this work taught me:** Interviews are conversations between humans, not data collection exercises. This project deepened my curiosity about how we foster care and responsibility toward others in shared spaces.
