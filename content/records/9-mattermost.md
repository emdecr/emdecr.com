_Crafting and evolving a complex web and GTM system_

---

When I joined Mattermost in 2021, I thought I was signing up for a familiar job:

Build web pages. Improve the site. Support Marketing.

Yes, that happened.

But the deeper story is that Mattermost was where I learned how much *a website is not just a website*. It’s a living interface between product, mission, customer trust, and revenue.

---

## The role I thought I was taking vs. the one I actually took

The role on paper was web development.

In practice, I was the **sole web developer on the Marketing team**, responsible for the health, evolution, and reliability of the entire marketing site.

The work quickly became a blend of:

- front-end architecture and delivery  
- ownership of a large WordPress codebase  
- coordination across Marketing, Product, Design, and Sales  
- shipping high-visibility work under real deadlines  
- deep exposure to Marketing Ops (Marketo, Salesforce, procurement)  
- launching GTM programs like webinars and inbound-led outbound  
- improving conversion flows, including self-serve initiatives  

Over time, I moved from executing requests to owning initiatives end-to-end — from scoping and sequencing to launch and iteration.

---

## Three moments that defined the chapter

### 1) The rebrand launch

One of the defining projects of my time at Mattermost was a website redesign and rebrand delivered on a tight, non-negotiable timeline.

The scope was substantial: **hundreds of pages**, multiple templates, and **dozens of reusable blocks and components** within a single, high-traffic WordPress site.

I led development as the primary owner and managed two external developers during the most intensive phase, tapering their involvement as the system stabilized. This sharpened skills beyond implementation: delegation, review, unblocking, and maintaining quality across contributors.

The work was high-leverage because it touched everything at once:
UX, content structure, navigation logic, SEO risk, analytics, conversion flows, performance, and stakeholder expectations.

Despite the pressure and complexity, the launch went smoothly. The result of careful sequencing, tight coordination, and thorough QA long before launch day.

---

### 2) Building and pushing a self-serve flow with Product

I partnered with Product to support a new self-serve experience.

On the marketing side, I overhauled CTAs across all primary entry points to guide users toward this new flow. The marketing site became the **first meaningful touchpoint** — capturing the user’s email and handing them off cleanly to the product experience to continue trial setup.

This pulled me out of “marketing site land” and into product-adjacent thinking: how users move, where friction appears, and how early decisions shape downstream outcomes.

---

### 3) Inbound-led outbound (a net-new workflow, shipped quickly)

This project wasn’t just a build – it was a **net-new workflow**.

We implemented Clearbit to identify companies visiting the site, used Clay to find ICP-aligned contacts, layered in manual and automated enrichment, and routed qualified leads into Outreach sequences.

My role sat at the intersection of site instrumentation, data flow, tooling integration, and operational clarity.

The goal was speed without chaos: shipping quickly while keeping the workflow understandable and maintainable for the team.

---

## The unexpected education: marketing ops and revenue systems

Mattermost is where I gained hands-on exposure to the machinery behind modern GTM teams.

I integrated and maintained tools including Marketo, Salesforce, Clay, ZoomInfo, Outreach, and Clearbit to support lead capture, enrichment, routing, and campaign execution.

Along the way, I learned what implementation looks like in practice: procurement constraints, stakeholder alignment, imperfect data, and building systems that hold up under change.

---

## What I’m taking with me

Mattermost strengthened parts of me that I now treat as core skills:

- Owning high-stakes redesigns without sacrificing quality  
- Serving as the sole technical owner of a large marketing surface  
- Building trust across Marketing, Product, Design, and Sales  
- Translating fuzzy goals into concrete, shippable plans  
- Implementing GTM workflows end-to-end  
- Managing external developers while keeping execution cohesive  
- Operating inside ambiguity without freezing  

Most importantly, Mattermost expanded my definition of what “web developer” can mean and other roles and responsibilities it could evolve into.
