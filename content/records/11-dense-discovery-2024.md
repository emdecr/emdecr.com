I was delighted to be featured in **[Dense Discovery](https://www.densediscovery.com/issues/277)** Issue 277 with a *Worthy Five* — five small recommendations offered as prompts rather than prescriptions. Dense Discovery is a newsletter written by Kai Brach, the creator of *Offscreen*, one of my favourite independent magazines – I also did a similar feature in [their 23rd issue](https://www.offscreenmag.com/issues/23).

My selections reflect themes I keep circling lately: attention, patience, and learning when to loosen my grip. They range from a grounding question I return to in moments of spiraling (*“Is this worth your energy?”*), to the imaginative afterlives of *Sum: Forty Tales from the Afterlives* by David Eagleman, to a Pangasinan phrase my mum loves that roughly means *find a way* — usually delivered with a fond eye roll.

I also shared a piece of advice from Jocelyn K. Glei that I’m still practicing: allow things the time and space they need to ripen.

Small things, gently offered. You can read the full *Worthy Five* in **[Dense Discovery Issue 277](https://www.densediscovery.com/issues/277)**.
